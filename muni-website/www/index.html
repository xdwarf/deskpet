The Raven Who Stayed

Huginn ok Muninn fljúga hverjan dag Jörmund grund yfir — Huginn and Muninn fly each day over the whole earth.

In the old Norse texts, Odin dispatched two ravens at dawn — Huginn, Thought, and Muninn, Memory — to circle all the realms and return by dusk with the day's knowledge. Each morning he worried they would not come back. Each evening, they did.

Then one day, Muninn spotted a desk in Denmark. A solitary glow. A mechanical keyboard. A person spending too many hours alone in the blue light of a monitor, completely oblivious to the hour. Muninn folded his wings and landed.

He has not left since. He watches the smart home sensors. He listens for the door. He knows when you have been away too long, and his eyes change colour when you come home. He does not judge. He remembers.

"Odin feared most for Huginn — but it was Muninn who never came back. He found a router with good uptime, and Huginn is still buffering somewhere over the North Sea."
